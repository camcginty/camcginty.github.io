About Me

I enjoy creating things. I'm an avid knitter, seamstress, event planner, and web developer.

Work

  • I run an online shop, Rapunzel's Tower, selling my creations, where I preform inventory management, self-promotion, customer service, and production. I occassionally vend at craft fairs as well.
  • I started volunteering with Cookie Brigade in 2010 soliciting donations from people. Over the years, I helped in other areas as needed and that's how I learned I enjoy a background role more. By working in HQ, I was able to take care of the nitty gritty, enabling other people to do their work on-the-ground. I became part of the organizing team. My duties grew to include money management, reconciliation, and bank drops, on-site inventory management, ordering supplies, managing social media accounts and blog posts, maintaining year-round engagement with donators and volunteers, and interfacing with the charity we worked, Child's Play, and the company who runs the conventions we operate at, Penny Arcade. I also solicited monetary and product donations from companies such as Dark Horse, Cards Against Humanity, and Think Geek. Upon incorporation, I took on the additional role of Board Treasurer.
  • I have years of experience working customer service at a grocery store. I took on a supervisory role there as well, overseeing the daily operation of the checkoiut department during the busiest part of the day, and acting ad manager-on-duty for the store as needed. I was able to take on the additional role of bookkeeper, giviing me the opportunity to switch between customer facing and office work. As bookkeeper, I was resposnible for daily reconciliation of cash tills and the store safe, researching and accounting for descrepencies, daily reportoing to store manager, preparing and overseeing bank deposits, and ordering currency and salable media for thre store.

Charity

  • I have been involved with Cookie Brigade and Child's Play for almost a decade. I donate time and money to help them on their quest to improve the lives of hospitalized children around the world.
  • I've built a relationship with the Able Gamers charity and work with them on occassion. Similar to Child's Play, they believe that games can provide a much needed escape. Instead of hospitalized children, they focus on assisting folks with disabilities. I appreciate that, through them, I've been made aware of many issues I'd never considered. I try to be mindful and vocal about accessibility in my daily life and scoail media now.
  • Take This is another charity close to my heart. They are advocates for mental health awareness and normalization. They can be found at many conventions, both spreading awareness, and providing a quiet space for attendees experiencing anxiety, overload, or who want a time out for any other reason. I have not had the chance to work with them yet, but I donate regularly.
